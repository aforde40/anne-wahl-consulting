That combination of investment strategy, asset management, and fund operations doesn't come up often in one person. Having lived on each side of the table changes how you approach problems. You ask different questions. You understand what the person across from you actually needs.

I recently launched an independent consulting practice working with GPs, investment teams, and financial operations professionals who need senior level support on a project or ongoing basis. The work spans financial modeling, performance reporting, waterfall and carried interest modeling, investor reporting, model QA, and middle and back office operations.

AI is central to how I work but my starting point is always the process. A lot of what teams accept as standard workflow was built around tools and limitations that no longer exist. Before automating anything, I make sure we're solving the right problem.

The example below illustrates a recent incentive fee model engagement. Each project is scoped individually — but the approach is always the same: collaborative, transparent, and built around your team's long-term ownership of the output.

Engagement Objective In response to increasing complexity and operational risk in incentive fee calculations, this engagement designed and implemented a scalable, well-controlled incentive fee model — improving accuracy and transparency, and enabling sustainable long-term ownership by the Accounting team.

Project Timeline

Week 1

Assessment & Alignment

Review current processes, models, and assumptions. Confirm requirements and success criteria with the Accounting team.

Weeks 2–3

Design & Model Development

Build the incentive fee model — standardized, transparent, and designed to roll forward efficiently period to period.

Week 4

Testing & Validation

Validate calculations, assumptions, controls, and outputs. Refine based on Accounting team feedback and edge case testing.

Week 5

Finalization & Handoff

Complete documentation, establish governance and controls, and transition full ownership to the Accounting team.

Ongoing

Optional Maintenance

Available to update models for new deals, capital calls, or operational changes as your fund evolves.
